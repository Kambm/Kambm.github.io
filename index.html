About Me

I am currently a Research Associate at the Chan Zuckerberg Biohub, focusing on providing theoretical and computational accounts of cellular and tissue-scale biophysical processes.

Previously, I was an undergraduate at the University of Washington, where I obtained a B.S. with a double major in Computer Science and Physics. During my time at the University of Washington, I worked with J. Nathan Kutz and Steve Brunton on dynamical systems inference and Koopman operator theory. I've also spent time at the Allen Institute for Brain Science, where I worked on developing machine learning models for predicting neural activity in response to visual stimuli.

When not thinking about math and physics, you can often find me playing the piano or the cello, running, or hiking around in the mountains.
